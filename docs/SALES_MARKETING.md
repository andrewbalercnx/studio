## Executive Summary

StoryPic creates AI-generated personalized children's storybooks featuring the child's own photos. Our go-to-market strategy leverages:

- **Emotional product appeal** - Parents and grandparents love personalized gifts
- **Gift-driven purchasing** - High intent around birthdays, Christmas, christenings
- **Word-of-mouth potential** - Shareable, Instagram-worthy product
- **B2B opportunities** - Schools, nurseries, corporate gifts

**Target**: £250K+ annual revenue within 24 months.

## Phase 1: Foundation & Validation (Months 1-3)

### Objective
Validate product-market fit with minimal spend, gather testimonials, and refine the offering.

### Targets
- 50-100 paying customers
- £2-5K revenue
- 10+ testimonials with photos
- Customer satisfaction 4.5+/5

### Tactics

#### Friends & Family Launch
- [ ] Create list of 50+ parents/grandparents in network
- [ ] Design "founding customer" offer (50% discount for feedback)
- [ ] Personal outreach via WhatsApp/email
- [ ] Follow up for reviews and testimonials

#### Local Community Testing
- [ ] Identify 5 local nurseries/primary schools
- [ ] Create school partnership proposal
- [ ] Pilot "class book" program with 2-3 schools
- [ ] Capture video testimonials

#### Social Proof Foundation
- [ ] Set up Instagram account
- [ ] Set up Facebook page
- [ ] Create content calendar (daily posts)

  - [ ] "Custom storybook with child's photo"

### Channel Notes

#### Social Media
- **Instagram**: Primary channel for visual content, unboxing videos, customer photos
- **Facebook**: Parent groups, targeted ads, community building
- **TikTok**: Younger parents, viral potential, behind-the-scenes content

#### Paid Advertising
- Start small (£20-50/day), test extensively before scaling
- Focus on video content - performs better for emotional products
- Retargeting is crucial - long consideration cycle for gifts

#### Email Marketing
- Welcome sequence critical for conversion
- Segment by: gift giver vs parent, occasion, child age
- Trigger emails: cart abandonment, browse abandonment, post-purchase
